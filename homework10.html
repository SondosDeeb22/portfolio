Conclusion

The survey results reveal that the majority of participants (86.4%) have used an online shopping website, and of those, 63.6% have made at least one purchase. Shopping frequency varied widely, with roughly equal groups placing orders once a week, once a year, or rarely, suggesting a diverse range of engagement levels with e-commerce platforms.

Demographically, respondents were evenly split by gender (50/50), with the largest age group being 18–25 (36.4%). Educational background skewed towards higher education, with 86.4% having attended college or graduate school, which may partly explain the comfort with digital commerce.

Platform preferences were broad, reflecting the global diversity of online marketplaces available today. Amazon, Shein, and Trendyol appeared frequently, highlighting both global giants and region-specific platforms serving the respondent base.
